Computer Hardware and Software ○ Portfolio #4

Analysis/Reflection

There is no doubt that computers, both their hardware and software, are amazing inventions. They can do things that people in medieval times would have thought were ‘magic’ or ‘sorcery.’ Many people don’t realize that a device allowing us to talk to someone across the globe can fit in our pockets—an idea that would have seemed silly or impossible in the 16th century. It’s incredible how quickly computing and technology have advanced in just a few decades. Computers are now everywhere. As shown in earlier slides, people in the past could not have imagined technology being used in medical practices to the extent it is today, since medicine is such a human-centered field. As a computer science student, I might be biased, but I think computers are among the greatest discoveries of humanity, alongside the first fire made by cavemen and Benjamin Franklin’s experiments with kites and electricity. It’s amazing that machines that once filled entire rooms to perform simple calculations can now fit on our desks. This progress is due to our ongoing efforts to improve hardware, which has greatly increased computing power. We even have a trend called Moore’s Law, which says that the number of transistors on a chip goes up while the price goes down every two years. I recently saw a video suggesting that the industry is reaching a point where this trend is starting to level off, but it’s still impressive that we have come this far and made powerful computers widely available. In summary, the development of computers is one of humanity's most significant achievements, much like discovering fire or electricity. As technology becomes part of every aspect of our lives, their future is full of possibilities, even as we face challenges in keeping up the rapid pace of change. The story of computing shows our endless curiosity and drive for innovation, constantly reshaping our world and society as we know it.
